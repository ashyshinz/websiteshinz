13 May 2024 Coping Anxiety

All of us have occasionally experienced tension or anxiety, and most of the time, this is normal. However, having struggled with general anxiety for most of my life, I've had to learn coping mechanisms for my emotions and thoughts. Finding the reason behind your feelings is the first step in managing these emotions. Although it may seem obvious, our minds frequently exaggerate even relatively insignificant events in order to make us feel powerless. I've discovered that the majority of the time when I experience anxiety, it comes from irrationality and is typically caused by worry about unrealized fears. I first try to slow my breathing and practice reflective thinking when my mind starts to race and I start to lose control. I first ask myself what specifically is worrying me, and then I determine whether the worry is for the now or for the future. Almost invariably, it begins with "What if..." and is a worry about the futureâ€”something that hasn't happened yet. What happens if I'm disliked by everyone? What happens if I don't pass this class?What happens if my parents don't like who I am? â€œ In situations like this, I remind myself that worrying about it now won't make the hurt or anxiety I'll experience later, when that "what if" really happens, go away. Essentially, it all comes down to remaining present. Since life consists only of the moments you are currently experiencing, anxiety can be greatly reduced when you start to focus entirely on each one of these times rather than on the past or future.
